Lack of Convergence Reference

Supplemental impairment assessment tool. Not scored as SFST clues in this demo.

Quick Reference

  • Ask whether the subject is wearing glasses or contacts.
  • Advise the subject that you are going to check their eyes.
  • Have the subject stand with feet together and arms at their side.
  • Instruct the subject to keep their head still and follow the stimulus with both eyes.
  • Move the stimulus slowly toward the bridge of the nose.
  • Observe whether both eyes converge.
  • Repeat as needed to confirm observations.

Observation Focus

  • Eyes converge.
  • Lack of convergence observed.
  • Rebound / eyes drift outward.
  • Unable to follow stimulus.

Alternative Test Battery Reference

Supplemental tests used as additional impairment indicators. These are not clue-scored in this demo.

Use Criteria

  • 50 pounds overweight.
  • Over 65 years of age.
  • Injury claimed.

Alphabet

  • Explain alphabet test instructions.
  • Observe whether subject completes the test.
  • Note errors, omissions, or improper sequence.

Finger Count

  • Explain finger count instructions.
  • Subject attempts 1, 2, 3, 4 sequence touching each finger to thumb.
  • Subject attempts 4, 3, 2, 1 sequence touching each finger to thumb.
  • Observe improper count, missed finger-to-thumb contact, or other errors.

Rhomberg

  • Explain Rhomberg instructions.
  • Subject closes eyes and tilts head back.
  • Hands remain at side.
  • Stop after approximately 30 seconds.
  • Observe swaying, balance issues, or incorrect time estimate.

Hand Clap

  • Explain hand clap instructions.
  • Subject counts to 10.
  • Front and back of palm count as one number.
  • Observe improper count or improper hand movement.

Nose Touch

  • Explain nose touch instructions.
  • Subject closes eyes.
  • Right / left command given.
  • Observe missed nose touch, wrong finger, wrong hand, or other error.

PBT / Final Indicators

  • Document PBT result or refusal if applicable.
  • Document whether impairment indicators appear consistent with alcohol, drugs, or both.
